Historical Background of Kingdom Mission Society & KEME

Our History (2004 - 2024)

"WISDOM IS TRUE LIFE, WEALTH AND HONOR, PROVERBS 3:13-20."

Kingdom Educational Mission Euro (KEME) provides educational support for students from war-torn regions of Burma, particularly Kachin and other ethnic regions, after six decades of civil war.

It is a partnership mission of the Kingdom Mission Society (KMS), Kachin Baptist Churches, Kuki Churches of Manipur, Baptist Churches of Nagaland, Singhpo Baptist Community, and the Churches of Assam and Arunachal.

KEME is rooted in Christian and democratic principles and seeks to instill in every student faith in the Bible, Christian ethics, spiritual maturity, and leadership.

KEME welcomes all students regardless of denomination or ethnicity and is funded by donations and student fees, with scholarships available for poorer students,, and provides access to primary through higher education in Burma and neighboring countries.

KEME emerged out of the Kachin Baptist Churches and resurrects a mission school tradition that flourished from the late nineteenth century until 1962 when the mission schools were outlawed.

The Kachin, Shan, and Kayah ethnic regions have been particularly damaged by Burma's ongoing warfare. Burmese troops have targeted Kachin churches and about 40,000 students have had even a basic education disrupted.

Academic Explanation of KEM Mission Components

1. Faith-Based Educational Vision

KEM’s foundation lies in Christian principles and biblical ethics, which guide its vision of educational transformation. The organization seeks to revive the legacy of Christian mission education in Myanmar by fostering spiritually mature, ethically grounded, and academically capable individuals. These values are intended to prepare young people to lead their communities with integrity and a strong moral compass. This aligns closely with the legacy of missionaries such as Adoniram Judson and William Carey, who served in the region.

2. Empowerment through Inclusive Education

KEM aims to offer access to quality education for students from diverse ethnic backgrounds, especially those affected by civil conflict. By running mission schools and partnering with local churches and educational institutions, KEM provides a learning environment where inclusivity and respect are core values. The mission is open to students regardless of ethnicity or denomination, emphasizing a democratic and just approach to education that uplifts marginalized populations.

3. Leadership Development and Civic Engagement

Leadership formation is a central pillar of KEM’s strategy. The organization cultivates future leaders through academic and vocational programs that encourage critical thinking, moral decision-making, and civic responsibility. KEM's goal is to nurture a new generation of ethical leaders who can contribute to democratic development and peacebuilding in Myanmar’s complex socio-political landscape.

Learn More
4. Humanitarian Relief and Community Health

Recognizing the immediate needs of vulnerable populations, KEM integrates humanitarian support into its mission. This includes establishing and supporting elderly care centers, orphanages, and emergency relief care facilities for people affected by ongoing conflicts. These services address both short-term survival needs and long-term well-being, reinforcing the organization’s holistic approach to human development.

5. Vocational and Language Training

In response to regional labor market needs and international opportunities, KEM provides vocational education and language training (Finnish and English). These programs equip students with employable skills and increase their mobility and readiness for higher education or careers abroad. This practical focus supports KEM’s broader objective of creating sustainable livelihoods and self-sufficient communities.

6. Global and Local Partnerships

KEM actively seeks collaborations with both local and international partners to enhance its reach and effectiveness. Its programs are designed to align with the missions of educational institutions, churches, and humanitarian organizations. By fostering partnerships with stakeholders who share similar values—such as educational equity, faith-based service, and sustainable development—KEM ensures mutual benefit and increased impact through resource sharing, cultural exchange, and programmatic innovation.

Student Life in Finland

Student life in Finland is peaceful, safe, and well-balanced. Finland offers a calm environment, modern universities, and strong support systems for international students. Daily life is organised, reliable, and friendly, helping students focus on learning and personal growth. Finland provides affordable meals, safe cities, and a supportive university structure that helps students adjust easily.

Finnish campuses are known for

  • Active student unions and clubs
  • Events, festivals, and cultural celebrations like Vappu
  • Friendly student communities, including English-speaking groups
  • A relaxed and welcoming atmosphere

    • Students enjoy activities such as sitsit (formal dinners), movie nights, game events, and social gatherings.

    Education and lifestyle in Finland are designed to be balanced

  • Low hierarchy between students and teachers
  • Freedom to study independently
  • Flexible learning environments
  • Nature always close by—forests, lakes, and fresh air

    • Student life is both vibrant and calm, giving space to grow academically and personally.

    Working Life in Finland for Students

    Working life in Finland is built on fairness, equality, and balance. Finnish workplaces are known for trust, punctuality, and respect. Finland also offers generous work rights for international students.

    International students can work

  • Up to 30 hours per week during the academic term
  • Full-time during holidays
  • These are some of the highest student work rights in Europe

    • Finnish universities often provide job portals and guidance to help students find suitable employment opportunities.

    Finnish workplaces are known for

  • Flat hierarchy – everyone is treated equally
  • Direct communication
  • Strong work–life balance (5 PM means 5 PM)
  • Low stress and low burnout
  • Finns value honesty, teamwork, and reliability.

    • Students from outside the EU/EEA can also work without limits if the job is a required part of their degree (internships, traineeships).

    Students often find work through

  • Internships
  • Summer jobs
  • Thesis projects with companies
  • Networking events
  • Online job platforms like Työmarkkinatori and Work in Finland

    • While many jobs are available in English (especially in IT, research, and international companies), learning Finnish improves job opportunities in

  • Healthcare
  • Customer service
  • Education
  • Public services
  • Even basic Finnish (A2–B1) gives a big advantage.

    • Finland offers what many students look for

  • Safe and modern living
  • High-quality education
  • Strong work rights
  • Clear pathway to long-term residence
  • Supportive and friendly community

    • Student life is meaningful, balanced, and full of opportunities—both during your studies and after graduation. Finland truly gives students the space to study, work, and grow.

    Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

    1. Who can join KEME programs?

    Anyone who is interested! Students, newcomers to Finland, Germany and Czech Republic, working adults, youth, and international learners can join our language, music, and exam courses.

    2. Do I need any experience to start?

    No. All our programs start from basic level. Beginners are welcome.

    3. What languages can I learn at KEME?

    You can learn Kachin, Finnish, German, and Czech from beginner to advanced levels.

    4. Do you offer exam preparation?

    Yes. We provide IELTS and SAT classes with practice tests and exam strategies.

    5. Can your programs help me study in Finland or Europe?

    Yes. Our language training and pathway guidance support students who want to study in Finland, Germany, Czech Republic, and other European countries.

    6. Do you help newcomers to Finland?

    Yes. We offer Finnish language classes and integration support to help newcomers adjust to daily life in Finland.

    7. What humanitarian services does KEME provide?

    We serve in:

    • Environmental safety
    • Orphanage care
    • Elderly care
    • Emergency relief
    • Humanitarian support
    • Sustainable development

    8. Where do the profits go?

    All profits are reinvested to support our humanitarian and community projects.

    9. Do you offer music courses?

    Yes. We offer song composition, guitar, and piano lessons for all ages.

    10. How can I register?

    You can contact KEME through our website, social media, or visit us directly for registration support.

    11. Do you offer scholarships?

    Yes. KEME offers scholarships depending on availability. Some courses may have partial support, and in some cases, students in need may receive fee reductions or free-learning options. Scholarships are given based on:

    • Need
    • Commitment
    • Program availability

    You can ask our team during registration to check if any scholarship or support is open for you.

    12. What are the program fees?

    Our program fees vary depending on:

    • What you want to study
    • The level (beginner, intermediate, advanced)
    • Whether the class is group or private
    • Course duration

    Some courses are free to learn, especially for community support or humanitarian learners. We will explain your exact fee when you register, based on your chosen program.

    13. Do you offer refunds?

    Yes, but only in certain situations. Refunds are possible only when:

    • A student cancels before the course starts
    • A class is cancelled by KEME
    • There is a special approved reason (health, emergency, etc.)

    Refund requests must be made directly to our office or through our support team.

    14. How does the refund process work?

    The process is simple:

    1. You contact KEME and explain why you want a refund.
    2. Our team reviews your case.
    3. If approved, the refund will be issued to the same payment method.

    Refund time depends on your bank or payment service.

    15. Are all programs refundable?

    Not always:

    • Some programs are non-refundable once the class begins
    • Free courses do not include refunds (because no fee was paid)
    • Long term programs may offer partial refunds before the course starts

    We will explain the refund rule for your specific course during registration.
